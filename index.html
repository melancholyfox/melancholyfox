##### CLUTCH DIAGNOSIS PT.2 #####

Some of the factors that can reduce the service life of a clutch include:

1. Extensive stop and go city driving, especially in hilly terrain.

2. Frequent severe duty, such as towing or hauling.

3. Contamination ( such as oil leaking from the engine or transmission )

4. The last one is Driver misuse or abuse.

driver abuse could mean like if you are whacking the clutch with a hammer, though it might also be talking about verbal abuse, like if you often yell at your clutch. Driver misuse could also be talking about riding the clutch, where you drive with one foot on the clutch pedal at all times. That can cause premature bearing failure or slippage.

Riding the clutch can also refer to the driver allowing excessive slippage of the clutch from a stop between gears, especially at a high RPM, or using the clutch to hold the vehicle in place when stopped facing uphill.

In racing, engines are often revved to a high RPM and the clutch pedal is released suddenly for a quick take-off. This is called "dumping" or "dropping" the clutch. While this is good for racing, it does not promote long clutch life.

Clutch life is most easily extended by slipping the clutch as little as possible when starting out or changing gears. The clutch should be smoothly slipped at a low RPM when pulling away from a stop, and engaged fully as soon as possible without lugging the engine, which is loading the engine too heavily for the RPM, which stresses drivetrain components.
